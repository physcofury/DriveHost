DriveHost — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

DriveHost is a free, open-source Minecraft mod that enables peer-to-peer world hosting using Google Drive as shared storage.

What data we access

DriveHost requests access to Google Drive with the drive.file scope. This scope grants access only to files that the mod itself creates — it cannot read, modify, or delete any other files in your Google Drive.

What we store on Google Drive

  • world.zip.enc — your Minecraft world save, encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a password you set. It is unreadable without that password.
  • session.json.enc — encrypted session state (who is hosting, connected players). Also AES-256-GCM encrypted.
  • keycheck.json — a salt and HMAC hash used to verify your password locally. It does not contain your password or encryption key.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not collect, transmit, or store your data on any server we operate.
  • We do not share your data with any third party.
  • We do not access any Google Drive files outside of the DriveHost folder you designate.
  • We do not store your Google account credentials — OAuth tokens are cached locally on your own machine only.

OAuth tokens

After you authorise DriveHost via Google Sign-In, a refresh token is stored locally at .minecraft/config/drivehost/tokens/ on your own computer. This token is never uploaded or transmitted anywhere.

Open source
